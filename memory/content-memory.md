# Content Memory

## Purpose
A running record of the brand's narrative and content output — the origin story, headline systems, and what content actually shipped — so CBO-Agent extends a continuous story instead of writing a new one every time.

## Stored Information
- Origin story and narrative arc currently in use, and what changed since the last revision
- Headline systems and copy frameworks currently approved, with examples of what performed well
- Content calendar history: what shipped, on which channel, and the cadence that was actually sustained vs. planned
- Content taxonomy — categories, pillars, and how new content gets classified into them

## Update Rules
- Update the origin story only on a deliberate narrative revision, not incrementally — log the full before/after, since a half-changed story is worse than the old one
- Log shipped content against the calendar that was planned, including gaps, so cadence claims are based on what happened, not what was scheduled
- New copy frameworks get an entry only once they're approved for reuse, not on every individual piece of copy

## Usage
Read by CBO-Agent before any storytelling, copywriting, or content-system work. Read by CMO-Agent when building campaign content, to stay inside the approved narrative and copy frameworks rather than inventing new ones per campaign.

## Relationships
- Downstream of `brand-memory.md` (the story expresses the positioning decided there) and works alongside `voice-memory.md` (story is what's said, voice is how)
- Feeds `community-memory.md` when content informs community engagement
- Feeds `lessons-learned.md` when a content format underperforms its stated goal

## Examples
```
[2026-01-15] Origin story v1 set: founder-led narrative, "built it to
            solve our own problem first"
[2026-03-10] Headline framework approved: problem→stakes→solution,
            3-line max — adopted after A/B test outperformed feature-led
            headlines 2.1x on click-through
[2026-05-01] Content cadence: planned weekly, actually shipped
            biweekly for 6 weeks — capacity gap logged, not hidden
```
